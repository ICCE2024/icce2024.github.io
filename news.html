[17 Nov 2024] Tutorial 2 Session Pre-Registration

You are invited to participate in the Tutorial 2 session - Designing Learning Experiences for Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) Education using Minecraft!

Held in conjunction with ICCE 2024, this tutorial session aims to introduce the participants to the What-If Hypothetical Implementations in Minecraft (WHIMC), and provide a step-by-step guide on how learning experiences can be designed using WHIMC to nurture interest in Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM). WHIMC is a set of computer simulations built in Minecraft to engage students in “what if” questions such as “What if Earth had no moon?” or “What if Earth had a slightly colder sun?” WHIMC immerses students in these alternate versions of Earth, giving them opportunities to see how the planet’s geography and life forms would differ under these circumstances. It enables students to use science tools to collect data about these alternate Earths and record their observations.

The event will focus on going through each stage of learning experience development with the participants so they can succeed in achieving the learning outcomes and increased interest of their students in learning STEM concepts, skills and careers.

ICCE 2024 participants can register for FREE. Only 20 slots will be offered for the tutorial. We hope to see you there!

[22 Oct 2024] Interactive Event Pre-Registration for Educ-AI-tion: Bridging Divides with GenAI

Hello everyone! Magandang araw!

Secure your spot for the Interactive Event 01 - Educ-AI-tion: Bridging Divides with Educational GenAI! Held in conjunction with ICCE 2024, this Interactive Event focuses on three key areas:

    1. instructional design using GenAI,
    2. assessment creation using GenAI, and
    3. learning activity development using GenAI.

Through hands-on activities, participants will address the challenges and opportunities of implementing AI in diverse educational settings, considering factors such as technological infrastructure and resource availability. The workshop emphasizes ethical considerations in generative AI-enhanced education, including academic integrity, bias mitigation, and data privacy.

Participants will develop strategies for leveraging GenAI tools responsibly, ensuring cultural relevance and inclusivity in their educational practices. By providing practical experience and critical analysis, this workshop aims to equip educators with the skills to effectively integrate GenAI in ways that empower learners from diverse backgrounds, contributing to more accessible and culturally responsive educational technologies.

[10 Sep 2024] Call for Applicants for the APSCE Excellence Scholarship Award 2024

The Asia-Pacific Society for Computers in Education (APSCE) Excellence Scholarship Award (ESA), also formerly known as the APSCE Merit Scholarship Award (MSA), was established in 2007 as a financial aid and scholarship program to advance the mission of fostering and sustaining a vibrant community of young researchers within the region — a key objective of the Society.

The APSCE ESA Committee 2024 is calling for applicants and the application deadline is 16 September 2024 (Monday). Criteria for the ESA can be found here and all applications must be submitted online.

Applicants will be notified via e-mail of the result of their applications. Successful applicants will be required to formally inform the Program Committee of their decision to accept the Excellence Scholarship Award. Queries can be directed to both Chair and Co-chair.
